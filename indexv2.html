Why most people fail with AI

The tools aren't the problem.
The missing system is.

Every week another AI tool launches. Every week another person downloads it, plays with it for three days, and quietly gives up. Here's why.

01

They collect tools instead of building systems

ChatGPT for writing. Midjourney for images. Notion for notes. But none of these tools talk to each other. You're doing the integration manually — in your head — every single time.

02

They follow tutorial videos, not real users

That influencer reviewing 10 AI tools in 8 minutes hasn't used any of them seriously. The honest information — what actually costs money, what breaks, what works — lives with real builders.

03

They don't know what order to build in

Should you set up the automation before the content? The landing page before the email? Get the order wrong and you waste months building on top of something that needs to be rebuilt.

04

They don't know what it should actually cost

Tool subscriptions stack up fast. Most people are either overpaying for things they don't need or underpaying and hitting walls they can't debug. The economics matter.
